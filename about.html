Perpetua(l)

Stephanie Pakrul / 2016

About the exhibit

Perpetua(l) came about during the closing days of the iconic webcam portal CamWhores.com. While reviewing the 17,000 photos of my image archive, 200 or so cell phone cam photos stood out as a snapshot of mobile history. As far as I know, no one had yet implemented the process of taking a photo with a mobile phone and sending it to a webcam and gallery within a social network, over a cellular data connection. The first "camera phone" had been released only a year or so prior.

As these camera phones gained traction, I saw an opportunity to untether myself from the webcam cord. I picked up a flip phone to carry along with my Windows Mobile phone. It (barely) worked in Canada, but crucially, it had a good camera for the time.

Browsing through the hundreds of snapshots, a photo leapt out at me. receipt.jpg. I knew that shot. I had Instagrammed one with the same subject and composition months ago. I pulled them up side by side. A decade apart, they were different but the visual communication is strikingly similar.
